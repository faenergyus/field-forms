iAbout AVO inspections — what they are & how to log one

AVO = Audio, Visual, Olfactory. A walk-around inspection of a well or facility where the operator listens, looks, and smells for signs of a leak, odor, malfunction, or unusual condition. AVOs are a baseline LDAR (Leak Detection and Repair) compliance activity required by NMOCD 19.15.27 NMAC and the EPA NSPS OOOOa methane rules — every site shows up on this list on a cadence (typically monthly for production sites, weekly for higher-risk equipment).

What to look / listen / smell for

  • Odor: hydrocarbon vapor, H2S, sour gas, glycol, oily mist near tanks, separators, treaters, vents, thief hatches.
  • Leaks: oil/water/gas seepage at valves, flanges, fittings, pump packing, tubing connections; spray, sheen, drips, dampness, hissing or vapor wisps from atmospheric vents.
  • Noises: knocking, banging, squealing, grinding, abnormal compressor or pump-jack pitch, relief-valve discharge, gas blow-by.

How to log it on this page

  1. Check the rows you actually inspected. The list is everything currently open in dbo.avo_due. Use the Site filter to narrow by name, then uncheck anything you didn't visit.
  2. Flip Odor / Leaks / Noises to Yes only if you observed something. Default is No for all three.
  3. If you click Yes, a popup opens. Type a clear description (where, what, severity) and use the camera icon to attach a photo. Both go on the record.
  4. Submit checked (button at the top). The page picks a random business-hours timestamp within 3 days of each due date, spaced ≥10 min apart per pumper, and saves one inspection per row. The matching avo_due row is then closed.

Photo & description policy

  • Photo is required for any Yes if you want NMOCD/EPA to treat the record as audit-quality. Camera button opens straight to the rear camera on a phone.
  • Descriptions should be specific enough for the next inspector to find the same spot — e.g. "Slow drip on 2-inch flange at pump suction, north side of treater" beats "small leak".
  • Anything Yes also triggers a follow-up workflow downstream — log it accurately or the wrong crew rolls out.

Inspection timestamp

Inspection timestamp is auto-generated (random business-hours time within 3 days of the due date, spaced ~10 min apart per pumper). Fill the form out to the best of your knowledge.
